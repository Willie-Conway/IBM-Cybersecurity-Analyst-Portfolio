## Background: Understanding Cybersecurity Career Progression

Before beginning the lab exercises, it's helpful to understand how cybersecurity careers typically progress. The field offers multiple pathways for advancement, with professionals often moving between technical, managerial, and specialized roles as they gain experience and expertise.

**Entry-level roles** (0-3 years experience) focus on foundational security tasks: monitoring systems, responding to basic incidents, conducting audits, and learning the tools and techniques of the trade.

**Mid-level roles** (3-7 years experience) involve deeper technical expertise, project leadership, specialized skills like penetration testing, and greater autonomy in decision-making.

**Advanced-level roles** (7-15+ years experience) encompass strategic leadership, enterprise-wide security architecture, program management, and executive-level responsibilities.

The Cyber Seek Career Pathway tool visualizes these transitions, showing how professionals can move from one role to another as they advance in their careers.

## Exercise 1: Explore Career Pathway

In this exercise, you will explore the different career pathways from entry-level to mid-level and advanced-level careers using the Cyber Seek interactive tool.

### Step 1: Navigate to the Career Pathway Tool

1. Open your web browser and navigate to the Cyber Seek website
2. Locate and click on the **Career Pathway** tab at the top of the page

> **Note:** The Career Pathway tool provides an interactive representation of cybersecurity jobs and roles, showing common pathways and transitions from entry-level to mid-level and advanced-level careers.

![alt text](<../Screenshots/Cyber Crime Analyst.png>)

### Step 2: Explore an Entry-Level Role

1. On the Career Pathway interactive diagram, locate and click on an entry-level role such as **Cybersecurity Specialist**
2. Observe the connections that appear from this role. You will see pathways leading to several mid-level career options including:

   - Cybersecurity Analyst
   - Cybersecurity Consultant
   - Penetration and Vulnerability Tester
3. Take a moment to understand the visual representation. The lines connecting roles indicate common career progression paths that professionals in the field typically follow.

> **Example Observation:** You might see that a Cybersecurity Specialist often transitions to a Cybersecurity Analyst role as they gain more experience.

![alt text](<../Screenshots/Penetration Tester.png>)

### Step 3: Review Role Details

1. Scroll down the page to view detailed information about the Cybersecurity Specialist role
2. Review the following sections:

   - **Average Salary Information:** Note the salary range and median pay for this position
   - **Commonly Requested Certifications:** Identify which certifications employers most frequently require for this role
   - **Commonly Requested Skills:** Review the technical and professional skills that are in demand for this position
3. Take notes on certifications or skills that interest you or that you may want to pursue in your own career development

> **Example Observation:** You might notice that a Cybersecurity Specialist role commonly requires CompTIA Security+ certification and skills in network security, vulnerability assessment, and incident response.

### Step 4: Follow the Career Path to Mid-Level

1. Return to the interactive diagram and click on a mid-level role such as **Cybersecurity Analyst**
2. Observe the new connections that appear, showing advancement opportunities to advanced-level roles including:

   - Cybersecurity Manager
   - Cybersecurity Engineer
   - Cybersecurity Architect
3. This demonstrates how the same role can lead to multiple potential career directions—some more management-focused, others remaining technical, and some combining both elements

### Step 5: Explore Mid-Level Role Details

1. Scroll down to review detailed information about the Cybersecurity Analyst role
2. Pay attention to:

   - How the salary range compares to the entry-level role you viewed earlier
   - New certifications that become relevant at this career stage (such as CISSP or CISM)
   - Additional skills required as responsibilities increase
3. Note the progression in responsibility and compensation as professionals advance

### Step 6: Explore Additional Roles of Interest

1. Continue exploring the Career Pathway tool by clicking on other roles that interest you
2. For each role you explore:

   - Note the career level (entry, mid, or advanced)
   - Review the common pathways leading to and from the role
   - Examine the salary information
   - Identify key certifications and skills
3. Consider which roles align with your personal interests and career goals

> **Example Observation:** You might find that a Cybersecurity Engineer role often leads to a Cybersecurity Architect position, while also providing opportunities to transition into management roles.

![alt text](<../Screenshots/Training map.png>)

### Step 7: Explore Skills and Certifications

1. At the top of the page, click on the **Skills** or **Certifications** tab (depending on the website layout)
2. Explore the comprehensive lists of:

   - **Certifications:** Industry-recognized credentials that validate expertise in specific domains
   - **Skills:** Technical and professional competencies required for various roles
3. For certifications that interest you, note:

   - Which roles commonly require or prefer that certification
   - The experience level typically needed before pursuing the certification
   - Any prerequisites or recommended preparation
4. For skills that interest you, note:

   - Which roles require that skill
   - How the skill applies to daily responsibilities

> **Pro Tip:** Many cybersecurity professionals maintain a "skills roadmap" that identifies which certifications and skills they need to acquire to reach their desired career level. Use this information to begin planning your own professional development path.

## Exercise 2: Explore Interactive Map (Optional)

This exercise is optional but provides valuable insights into geographic job opportunities in the cybersecurity field.

### Step 1: Navigate to the Interactive Map

1. At the top of the Cyber Seek page, click on the **Interactive Map** tab
2. An interactive map of the United States will be displayed

> **Note:** If you don't see the Interactive Map tab, you may need to scroll down or look for a "Tools" or "Resources" section on the Cyber Seek homepage.

![alt text](<../Screenshots/Workforce Map.png>)

### Step 2: Explore State-by-State Opportunities

1. Hover your mouse over different states on the map
2. As you hover, observe the information displayed, including:

   - The number of current cybersecurity-related job openings in that state
   - (Depending on the data available) additional metrics about the cybersecurity workforce in that state
3. Click on a state that interests you to see more detailed information

### Step 3: Review State-Specific Details

1. After selecting a state, review the detailed information provided, which may include:

   - Current job openings by metropolitan area
   - Historical trends in cybersecurity employment
   - Top employers in the region
   - Salary information specific to that geographic area
2. Consider how geographic factors might influence your career planning:

   - Some regions have higher concentrations of cybersecurity jobs
   - Cost of living varies significantly by location
   - Remote work opportunities may expand options beyond your immediate area

![alt text](<../Screenshots/Cybersecurity SC.png>)

### Step 4: Compare Multiple States (Optional)

1. Select two or three different states to compare
2. Note any significant differences in:
   - Number of job opportunities
   - Types of roles in demand
   - Industry sectors represented (technology, finance, government, healthcare, etc.)

> **Career Planning Insight:** Understanding geographic job distribution can help you make informed decisions about where to focus your job search or whether to consider relocation for career opportunities. Many cybersecurity hubs have emerged beyond traditional technology centers, including Washington D.C. (government/defense), San Antonio (military/cyber command), and Atlanta (financial services/technology).

## Next Steps

Now that you have explored the Cyber Seek Career Pathway tool, consider these next steps in your cybersecurity career journey:

1. **Document Your Career Plan:** Create a personal roadmap identifying target roles at each career level and the certifications/skills needed to reach them
2. **Research Certification Requirements:** For certifications that interest you, research exam requirements, study materials, and preparation courses
3. **Build Foundational Skills:** Begin developing core competencies through online courses, home labs, or hands-on projects
4. **Connect with Professionals:** Use professional networking platforms to connect with individuals in roles that interest you and learn from their career paths
5. **Monitor the Job Market:** Regularly check job postings in your target geographic area to understand current employer demands and salary trends

**Tip:** Consider creating a personal cybersecurity career portfolio to document your progress, certifications, and projects as you advance in your career.
