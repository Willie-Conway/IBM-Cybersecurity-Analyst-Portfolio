# Cybersecurity Certifications: Value and Impact

## Introduction

Professional certifications play a critical role in the cybersecurity field, validating expertise, demonstrating commitment to the profession, and providing tangible career benefits. This article presents key findings from industry research on the value of cybersecurity certifications for both professionals and organizations.

---

## Key Research Findings

### Fortinet 2023 Cybersecurity Skills Gap Global Research Report

The Fortinet report provides valuable insights into how organizations view certifications and the impact of skills shortages on cybersecurity posture.

| Finding                                                                              | Implication                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **90% prefer to hire certified professionals** (up from 81% the previous year) | Certifications have become increasingly important in hiring decisions. Job seekers with relevant certifications have a significant competitive advantage over those without.                    |
| **90% willing to pay for employee certifications**                             | Organizations recognize the value of certified staff and are willing to invest in their development. This creates opportunities for professionals to obtain certifications funded by employers. |
| **95% of leaders with certified team members report positive results**         | The vast majority of organizations that invest in certifications see tangible benefits, validating the value of certification programs.                                                         |
| **68% face additional risks due to cybersecurity skills shortages**            | The skills gap directly impacts organizational security posture. Certified professionals help close this gap and reduce risk.                                                                   |

> **Key Insight:** The preference for certified professionals is nearly universal and growing. Organizations not only prefer hiring certified candidates but are also willing to invest in certifying their existing staff, recognizing the positive impact on security outcomes.

**Source:** Fortinet 2023 Cybersecurity Skills Gap Global Research Report

## Addressing the Skills Gap

The Fortinet report highlights a critical connection between certifications and organizational risk:

> **68% of organizations indicate they face additional risks because of cybersecurity skills shortages.**

Certifications help address this gap by:

1. **Validating competence**—ensuring certified professionals have verified knowledge
2. **Accelerating development**—structured certification paths fast-track skill acquisition
3. **Standardizing knowledge**—ensuring consistent understanding across the profession
4. **Demonstrating commitment**—certified professionals show dedication to the field
5. **Enabling specialization**—advanced certifications develop deep expertise in critical areas

## Making Certification Decisions

### Questions to Consider

| Question                             | Why It Matters                                             |
| :----------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------- |
| What roles am I targeting?           | Different roles require different certifications           |
| What level am I at?                  | Certifications are designed for specific experience levels |
| What domains interest me?            | Specialization areas have dedicated certification paths    |
| What do employers in my area prefer? | Regional and industry variations exist                     |
| What can I afford?                   | Certifications require financial and time investment       |
| Will my employer pay?                | Many organizations fund certifications (90% willing)       |

### Certification Planning Tips

1. **Start with foundational certifications** (Security+, Network+) before specializing
2. **Research job postings** for target roles to see which certifications appear most frequently
3. **Consider certification paths**—many vendors offer progressive certification series
4. **Plan for continuing education**—maintaining certifications requires ongoing effort
5. **Balance depth and breadth**—combine broad certifications (CISSP) with specialized credentials
6. **Time your certifications**—study while information is fresh, but ensure you meet experience requirements

## Conclusion

The research is clear: cybersecurity certifications provide significant value for both professionals and organizations. For individuals, certifications lead to better job performance, career advancement, and increased compensation. For organizations, certified staff reduce risk, improve security outcomes, and provide positive return on investment.

With 90% of employers preferring certified candidates and 85% of professionals pursuing certifications, the message is unmistakable: in cybersecurity, certifications are not optional—they are essential for career success and organizational security.

Whether you're entering the field or advancing your career, investing in relevant certifications is one of the most effective ways to demonstrate your expertise, differentiate yourself from competition, and achieve your professional goals.
