# Intellectual Property and Types of Confidential Information

**Estimated time needed: 5 minutes**

## Information Assets

An **information asset** is information or data that is of value to an organization. Examples include patient records, customer information, and intellectual property. Information assets can exist in various forms:

- Physically on paper, disks, or other media
- Electronically in databases and files

---

## Data, Information, and Insights

Understanding the hierarchy of data is essential for protecting information assets:

| Term                  | Definition                                                                     | Example                                                                                                                            |
| :-------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Data**        | Raw values and facts, usually collected by automated systems                   | Page visits, link clicks, monthly sales figures                                                                                    |
| **Information** | A summary of raw data that provides meaning                                    | Positive or negative results that occur after a specific change                                                                    |
| **Insights**    | Conclusions based on the analysis of information that drive business decisions | If a positive trend occurs after a manager changes store hours, the correct business decision would be to maintain those new hours |

**Data analytics** is the process of transforming raw data into meaningful information that can be used for decision-making.

---

## Intellectual Property (IP)

**Intellectual property** refers to creations of the mind that are generally not tangible. IP is often protected by copyright, trademark, and patent law.

### Types of Intellectual Property

| Type                           | Description                                                        |
| :----------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Industrial Designs**   | The ornamental or aesthetic aspects of a product                   |
| **Trade Secrets**        | Confidential business information that provides a competitive edge |
| **Research Discoveries** | Findings from scientific or business research                      |
| **Employee Knowledge**   | Specialized knowledge and expertise developed by employees         |

### Protecting Intellectual Property

**Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs)** are legally binding documents that companies use to prevent the sharing of sensitive information. NDAs establish confidentiality relationships between parties and protect proprietary information from disclosure.

---

## Digital Products

**Digital products** are non-tangible assets that a company owns. Examples include:

- Software applications
- Online music and streaming content
- Online courses and e-learning materials
- E-books and audiobooks
- Web elements (WordPress or Shopify themes)
- Source code, licenses, and activation keys

### Digital Product Protection

Companies must protect digital products from:

| Threat                        | Description                                                      |
| :---------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Piracy**              | Unauthorized copying and distribution of digital assets          |
| **Reverse Engineering** | Decompiling or analyzing products to replicate functionality     |
| **Hacker Attacks**      | Unauthorized access to source code, licenses, or activation keys |
| **Insider Threats**     | Employees or trusted individuals who misuse access               |

### Digital Rights Management (DRM)

**Digital Rights Management (DRM)** is code added directly to files that helps prevent digital assets from being copied or pirated. DRM controls how digital content is used, copied, and distributed. However, some tools can remove DRM code, making it an imperfect but valuable protection measure.

### Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA)

The **Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA)** is a U.S. copyright law that:

- Makes it illegal to bypass copy protections
- Prohibits the development or distribution of technology that helps bypass copy protections
- Provides legal recourse for copyright holders whose digital rights are violated

## Data-Driven Business Decisions

Data-driven business decisions help companies respond to real events and market conditions. Getting the correct information is key to effective data-driven decision-making.

### Types of Business Data

| Data Type                                 | Purpose                                                                                        |
| :---------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Sales and Marketing Data**        | Helps identify trends and customer interests                                                   |
| **Production and Fulfillment Data** | Helps identify productivity issues in manufacturing, billing systems, transportation, and more |

### Data Capture

**Data capture** is the collection of data from multiple sources and the secure storage of that data in relational databases or, more commonly, semi-structured data warehouses.

#### Data Capture Sources

| Source                   | Description                                                            |
| :----------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Server Logs**    | Records showing where customers browse and interact with websites      |
| **IoT Sensors**    | Data from connected devices in home appliances and business technology |
| **Surveys**        | Customer and employee feedback collected through questionnaires        |
| **Rating Systems** | User-generated ratings and reviews of products or services             |

### Data Correlation

**Data correlation** is the process of analyzing raw data points to find connections or links. For example, Netflix uses tools that compare searches, views, and ratings to predict which movies and shows will be successful on their platform. AI and machine learning algorithms can automate parts of this analysis, enabling more sophisticated pattern recognition at scale.

### Meaningful Reporting

**Meaningful reporting** is the presentation of analyzed information in ways that help people further analyze and interpret results. Reporting tools use captured and correlated data to provide:

- Charts and graphs
- Keyword searches
- Visual dashboards
- Trend analyses

These tools help companies achieve business insights that drive strategic decisions.

## Confidential Information

**Confidential information** is information that employees must keep secret. Organizations train employees to recognize and handle confidential information appropriately to maintain security.

### Information Classification

Companies rank information and files by how sensitive each one is. While classification systems vary by organization, four main types of confidential information should be universally protected:

### Four Types of Confidential Information

| Type                                                | Definition                                                                                                         | Examples                                                                                    |
| :-------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Personally Identifiable Information (PII)** | Any information that can be used to identify someone                                                               | Government ID numbers, birthdates, addresses, phone numbers                                 |
| **Company Confidential Information**          | Any information used to run a company                                                                              | Intellectual property, product designs, procedures, plans, employee records, financial data |
| **Customer Confidential Information**         | Information customers or partners provide to companies                                                             | PII, purchase histories, credit card information                                            |
| **Protected Health Information (PHI)**        | Any information added to a person's medical record during diagnosis or treatment that can be used to identify them | PII, medical history, prescription lists, photos, medical records                           |

### Examples of Improper Data Handling

| Scenario                                                                                | Issue                                      |
| :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------- |
| Entering a customer's credit card information into an unencrypted database              | Data exposure through lack of encryption   |
| Leaving a patient's medical file unattended at the front desk                           | Physical security breach of PHI            |
| Letting a work friend borrow your password to download files because they forgot theirs | Credential sharing and unauthorized access |

### Proper Handling of Confidential Information

| Best Practice                         | Description                                                                                                                   |
| :------------------------------------ | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Access Restriction**          | Limit access to only those who need the information to perform their job functions                                            |
| **Prevent Unauthorized Copies** | Do not allow unauthorized views or copies to be created                                                                       |
| **Secure Storage**              | Store information securely using encryption, firewalls, permissions, and other security controls                              |
| **Proper Disposal**             | Destroy any file copies that are no longer needed—not just discarding them, but ensuring complete destruction                |
| **Informed Consent**            | Get explicit consent before processing or storing information, including disclosure about how long the company will keep it   |
| **Strong Password Practices**   | Ensure employees create strong passwords that they do not write down or share, and that they change those passwords regularly |

## PII, PCI, and SPI

These three terms are often confused because they address similar concepts. In most non-legal situations, they are frequently used interchangeably. However, there are important distinctions:

### Comparison of PII, PCI, and SPI

| Term          | Full Name                           | Definition                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| :------------ | :---------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **PII** | Personally Identifiable Information | Information that identifies a person                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| **PCI** | Personal Customer Information       | Information that identifies and describes a customer. It includes many of the same data types as PII (name, address, contact information, account login, demographics) and can also include descriptive data like age, gender, job title, and marital status |
| **SPI** | Sensitive Personal Information      | Information that does not necessarily identify a person but can cause harm if made public                                                                                                                                                                    |

### Key Distinctions

- **PII** focuses specifically on identification—any data point or combination that can pinpoint an individual
- **PCI** encompasses a broader set of customer-related data, including both identifying and descriptive information
- **SPI** emphasizes the potential for harm—data that may not identify someone but could damage reputation, privacy, or security if exposed

## Summary

| Concept                            | Key Points                                                                                   |
| :--------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Information Assets**       | Valuable data including patient records, customer information, and intellectual property     |
| **Data to Insights**         | Raw data → Information → Insights drives business decisions                                |
| **Intellectual Property**    | Creations of the mind protected by NDAs, copyright, trademark, and patent law                |
| **Digital Products**         | Non-tangible assets requiring protection from piracy, reverse engineering, and theft         |
| **Confidential Information** | PII, Company Confidential, Customer Confidential, and PHI require strict handling procedures |
| **PII vs PCI vs SPI**        | PII identifies; PCI describes customers; SPI can cause harm if exposed                       |

Proper protection of intellectual property and confidential information is essential for organizational security, legal compliance, and maintaining customer trust. Organizations must implement comprehensive policies, technical controls, and employee training to safeguard these valuable assets.
